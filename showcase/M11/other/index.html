Building my personal website

With no konwledge and experience of building and designing a website, I started to learn HTML and CSS for the preparation of my personal website and showcase website. Through learning on the online shcool ("w3cschool") and reading some related articles about the skills of web design, I tried to design my own website and obtained the knowledge of web design, such as HTML5 & CSS3 and graphic design in web. I felt lucky that this kind of self-directed learning offered time and space for me to arrange my own learning schedule.

In the process of personal website design,  I got another chance to look back to my previous design works. I could rethink about all the works and what I have learnt from them. The precious works trained me for the basic skills of a designer. It was an explorative journey for me to figure out my on direction in design, and now I am growing up into a designer with professional skills.

Adapting to new environment

As it is my first semester of master here in TU/e, the first priority is to adapt myselft into the new education environment as well as the different culture. It was literally not an easy thing for me at the beginning, but I was gradually used to the new environment with time gonging by. I learnt the difference between western and estern culture through my owm experience with fellow students, coaches and other people around me. I really apreciate that these experience hepls me grow to maturity.

Being rational is the most obvious  character of westeren people, which leads them to do things driven by a clear goal. Coming from a total different background, I was confused by this kind of thinking and felt very hard to figure out the specific vision and identity of myself. Nevertheless, it turned out to be a good chance for me to seriously think about my plans and goals as a designer, and arrange the suitable learning activities for achieving them.

Another big difference is the way people get along with each other. Independence is vital. In terms of the study in TU, through the teamwork in this semester, although I encountered some trouble in the process, but I have learnt the way how students work in a team after the effrorts I made, which offers tips for me to collaborate beeter with the group in next semester. Besides, it widens my eyes on the understanding of the local culture and people.m Thannks to this experience, I could learn to think critically and behave with more easiness when treating different people.

The whole semester is a great opportunity for me, as a new international student here, to get familiar with the educational environment, from how to communicate with the coaches and students to how to use the facilities in the learning activities, such as the photostudio, all the machines in vertigo, the service desk, etc.

Design trip

I was very exciting to take this design trip to northern Europe. I got the opportunity to be close to the nordic desing which I have heard since I knew industrial design. It was really amazing to see these works of design and art. Especially in industrial design, I got to know how the design style is formed, how well they can integrate function and aesthetic, and even how the design thinking is carried in the design.

I have been appealed to the Schroder house and the famous red-bule chair. It is a fantastic design which perfectly make use of the space of the house. This classical design represents a kind of Stijl design style in Netherland.

After these design trips, I got a better understanding of the nordic desing and culture, Dutch design and culture. I would treat it a chance to broaden my horizon in design history.
