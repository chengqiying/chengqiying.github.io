Further building portfolio website

In last semester, I took a starting step to build the structure of home page of my personal website by learning HTML & CSS.  For a further development, firstly I continued studying web building language together with some knowledge of Java Script to complete the portfolio website. This time, I paid more attention on the web structure design than the web layout. I have been aware that the structure also has strong influence on users because it could led them have a clear understanding of  what the web intends to tell and easily find what they want to look at. This experience taught me the knowledge of user experience of on screen products and inspire my interests in it.

 

Besides, it is also a good time to rethink about my vision and identity, which are vital to convey in the portfolio. As it can be reflected from the projects presented in my portfolio, I have spent lots of efforts and interests in service design and interaction design both in my bachelor and master. My interest is still growing in service design with on screen products.

 

Reading articles of latest design related technology

Since started my study in TU/e, I have been curious about the  technology behind updated products. The technological environment in the industrial design apartment reminded the power and importance of technology. Therefore I gradually formed the habit to keep myself in post with the latest launched technologies which could bring new insights for designers to apply them in products. For example the newly  released tool for developers called Healthkit, which can make users' apps even more useful by allowing them to access their health data by allowing the incredible health and Ô¨Åtness apps to work together. This signal gave designer a wider space to create health products in the near future.

 

In the same time, I also read design articles to know the designers' thinking process behind the products, which is really beneficial to me to transfer my experience into systematic theories and methodologies, and better to interpret my design process and design thinking. Apart from that, the gained knowledge could support me to answer my questions in design process.

Coincidentally, I had the SDL week during the Milan Design Week, so I took a trip to Milan which is known for  its fashion and design. I subscribed the debate of Creativity after Hours held by the design company Frog, so I got the chance to be a live audience to absorb the various opinions of designers in Frog. The most impressive thing was the several turns of interpreting of two opposite key words in design. The designers gave their reasons why the worlds on their own side had a more vital role in design, like simple&complexity, science&art, etc. Through listening to this debate, on one hand, I had the view of what the design company is like, and on the other hand, I could updated my knowledge that these opposite aspects are not contradictory when applying them in different aspect in design cases. Also, I gained some inspirations about how to present the digital products and they helped me in preparation of final exhibition.

 

It is also worth mentioning that the exhibitions of art  and product design were really inspiring and well organized. They showed the special perspectives that different cultures had on design. For example, I gained the first time to get close to the South Korean design works and I was very surprised by their traditional crafting skills and underlying values.
