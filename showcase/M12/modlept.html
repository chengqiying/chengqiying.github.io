Module DB305

Persuasive Technology

Introduction

Goals: It is a one week module which targets on User Focus and Perspective. I would focus on learning how effective persuasive design can results in a change to a person's behaviors, attitudes or beliefs and how theories can help in that, which would bring a new angel to gain insights for user experience in design process. Starting a research project, I have been confused by the effect of the theory in a design process. As introduced that this module aims at bridging the theory and design, which is a pretty time for me to find the answer through  three iterations of this module.

 

Activities: We looked at different theories in persuasive technologies, that attempt to change attitude or behaviors by means of planned effects of interactive technology. We tried out the elaboration likelihood model to persuade arriving rush-hour commuters to take a desired path and make sure they don’t cross at the bottom of the stairs. In the final concept, we applied the Planned Behavior and Design with Intent theories in the  alternative contexts: the tunnel, the stairs and the platform in NS station in Eindhoven.

 

What I have learned

Gained understanding of persuasion

Bridge theory and design

Firstly, through the lectures, I got a holistic view of the introduced theories and mechanisms. Further in the three iterations, by implementing the theories in our concepts, I learned that persuasion is an efficient tool to reach the intended behavior changes in design process. Nevertheless, persuasive technology is definitely not a separate part of the design decision, it inherently happens in every stage of design activities as an ancillary element. It exists and impacts on in the detailed and specific segments of designs.

Secondly, in some extent, the approach to apply the persuasive technology is also a process of user research which based on a well understanding of the target user and the design goal. As I understand it now, it is a part of user study in a design case, which would has a big influence on the user group and the final intention. Taking advantage of persuasion is undoubtedly a big favor for the effect of the whole design.

For a better understanding of the persuasive theories and frameworks, we are required to pick a single theory or principle for each concept in the first two iterations. Every time we would struggle for how to put these theories into practice in design case because there are no specific steps in the paper to guide the approach. By exploring in the assignments, I came to understand that these theories can be used flexibly in inspiration, analysis and evaluation of concept generating, rather than just stick them in one single sector.

Compared with some papers I have read about phenomenology which transfer the rationale to concrete steps of experiments, I learned that the theories of persuasive technology are based more on psychological level, which results in the confusion of application in design process.

Reflection and Future development

On the whole, our final concept is an effective way to prevent overwhelming people on the platform. It is rigid in a way passengers are not offered with alternative choices, more like following instructions. However, behavior analysis might also be use not only in searching for design opportunities but also on how to implement the persuasions in the system. I also felt the communication of the theories and their integration into the concepts could be better and more transparent, so that the design for every step of the behavior could be more advisable and have effective influences.

 

Through the studying in this module, we aims at changing the user’s behavior to reach the design intent by using persuasive technology. In this way, we took lots of efforts to analyze the specific user and design for them. Therefore, I have meanly improved my competency in User Focus and Perspective in this one week study. Later, I took use of one of the theory called Design with Intent to stimulate the ideation part in my research project. And I found it taught me how to generate concept from a strategic level.
