Introduction

GHOSTs (generic, highly-organic shape-changing interfaces) are display surfaces made of malleable materials that can change into and retain arbitrary shapes so as to display output from the system or afford new actions. At the same time, GHOSTs allow users to deform, touch, or otherwise manipulate the shape of their display surface to provide input to the system.

Implementation

At first, we made a physical sketch to find out the appropriate construction of the box. Then, we used paper and cloth of various thicknesses to find out the smoothest one. After that, we made the first prototype to test the basic movement. In order to enhance the smoothness of movement, we change the construction of wheels. In the final prototype, we added another up board for the appearance of box and improve the tidiness of inside.

The Concept

The concept we chose was an rolling belt which was simple and suited the theme of changing behavior. The belt can be accelerated with touching. It goes fast in the same direction with touching when touching fast and slowly when touching slowly. It goes longer when touching with more pressure. It also can be stopped when touching again. And, there is a bonus sound effect when the belt stops in a high speed.
